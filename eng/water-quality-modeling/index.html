Acqualis Water Engineering

Environmental Risk Assessment.

Hydraulic · Sanitary · Environmental · Coastal Engineering

Assessment of layout alternatives for hydroelectric projects in river courses using water quality, hydrodynamic and sediment modeling.

Hydroelectric plants are one of the world's leading energy sources, using water resources for power generation. In addition, other energy production technologies use water in their processes, such as thermoelectric plants.  Acqualis Water Engineering has been working in the energy sector with environmental studies and support for basic engineering projects. Services include:

  • Environmental monitoring including installation, operation and maintenance of hydrometric stations for hydroelectric plants per ANA-ANEEL Joint Resolution No. 3;
  • Numerical modeling as support for basic hydroelectric projects, assessing layout alternatives and reduced flow studies;
  • Hydraulic, water quality and sediment transport modeling for environmental impact assessment in reservoirs and downstream reaches;
  • Assessment of trophic level potential in reservoirs and identification of dead zones;
  • Mathematical modeling for estimation of flooded vegetation (phytomass) in reservoirs;
  • Integrated Environmental Assessment of cascade hydroelectric projects using hydraulic, water quality and sediment transport modeling;
  • Dam-break study;
  • Studies of thermal plume dispersion and other effluent substances from thermoelectric plants in receiving water bodies.
